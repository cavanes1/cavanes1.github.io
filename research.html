Light-induced conical intersections

Principal investigator: Professor David R. Yarkony (deceased)

Conical intersections (CIs) play an important role in photochemistry, allowing for ultrafast radiationless decay in processes such as photodissociation. In addition to these natural CIs, light can give rise to seams of light-induced conical intersection (LICI), as the dipoleâ€“field interaction can significantly change the topography of the coupled potential energy surfaces. This work used an approximate Floquet Hamiltonian to explore the effect of LICIs on the photodissociation channels of ammonia, where a CI is known to play an important role.

Presented at the 29th Dynamics of Molecular Collisions Conference
Published in The Journal of Physical Chemistry Letters
Presented at the 2024 Molecular Interactions and Dynamics Gordon Research Conference


Photodetachment spectroscopy simulation

Principal investigator: Professor David R. Yarkony (deceased)

Simulated the anion ultraviolet photoelectron spectrum of tetrazolyl. A quasidiabatic Hamiltonian was used with a time-independent multimode vibronic coupling approach to yield the vibronic eigenstates and, by extension, the spectral intensity distribution.

Published in The Journal of Chemical Physics
Presented at the 28th Dynamics of Molecular Collisions Conference


Undergraduate research

Microscopic Characterization and Elemental Mapping of Catalytic Aerogels

Advisor: Professor Mary K. Carroll (2024 ACS president)

Synthesized metal-infused aerogels with a hydraulic hot press and operated advanced chemical instrumentation including SEM, AFM, XRD, and DSC in order to characterize microstructure. Developed and implemented novel sample preparation techniques that enabled nanometer-resolution electron microscopy of metal-infused aerogels previously inaccessible through conventional protocols.
