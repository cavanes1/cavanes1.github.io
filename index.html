About me

I am a theoretical and computational chemistry PhD candidate at Johns Hopkins University. I use high-accuracy quantum chemical calculations and machine learning to perform modeling and simulation of photochemical processes. I research laser cooling, molecular conical intersections, and nonadiabatic chemical dynamics. I also serve as a Safety Captain on the Johns Hopkins University Chemistry Student Safety Committee.

I graduated as valedictorian from Union College in Schenectady, NY with a double major in physics and chemistry. At Union, I studied metal-infused aerogels in the Union College Aerogel Lab, where I completed my Honors senior thesis. Check out the research tab to see the other research projects I've worked on.
