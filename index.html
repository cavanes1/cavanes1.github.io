About me

I am a quantum chemistry PhD candidate at Johns Hopkins University. I research molecular conical intersections and nonadiabatic dynamics of photochemical processes by performing quantum chemical calculations and dynamics simulations on supercomputers.

I graduated as valedictorian from Union College in Schenectady, NY with a BS in physics and in chemistry. At Union, I did research in the Union College Aerogel Lab, where I completed my Scholars (honors) project and my senior thesis. In addition, I have worked on the synthesis and characterization of gold nanoparticle-containing peptoid nanosheets. I also have research experience in experimental physics, where I developed an experimental setup to determine surface tension of liquids.
